Intro

I consider myself a self-motivated Data Scientist and Analyst who has a lot of passion for decoding the myth of data. I believe that Data Science and Statistics could be one of the approaches to answering even philosophical questions such as where the “mind” comes from. Data is not just numbers, they have information, and my job is to find them. With such motivations, I worked hard in the relevant field involving Data Science and Machine Learning and managed not only the application of various models but also the statistical algorithms behind them. I obtained a master's degree in Applied Data Science and Informatics from New York University and a bachelor's degree in Statistics. Through my past study and work experience, I am proficient in Python, R, and SQL.

Outside of the technical world, I am a musician who plays piano and guitar and a gamer. I love blues and funk music, and sometimes jam with my band. I also played a lot of video games and SoulsBorne series is my favorite.

Music and science are interrelated. Although seemingly different, they both are approaches to the ultimate answer of the universe. We often think rational and emotional are oppositions, but emotions are just subconcious logic, just as music chords follows Pythagoras's Golden Ratio, as the sounds of instruments are Fourier transformation of Sine Waves and as the history of Blues music is similar to Data Science: Learning from experiences, not formula.

The Anatomy of NYPD Crime Records: Race, Gender, Politics and More

This is a continuous study based on the two research me and my previous team have been done so far. In the first research we have proven the intergroup solidarity, felt and shared foreignness between Asians and HIspanics in the U.S. Then in the second research, we assumed they are under the similar social pressure and tried to testify a similar crime behavior patterns in NYC by checking the correlation based on their crime amounts and types distribution but we rejected the hypothesis. So in this project, our goal is to analyze and understand NYC Crime attributes in a more unbiased way by setting no hypothesis, targeting all races, using more data analysis techniques thus directly explaining the data
